About Us

Nike's story began in 1964 when Bill Bowerman, a track and field coach, and Phil Knight, a middle-distance runner, came together with a shared vision. They founded Blue Ribbon Sports, a small distributor of Japanese running shoes. The company started from humble beginnings, with Bowerman even experimenting with innovative shoe designs by pouring rubber into a waffle iron. In 1971, Blue Ribbon Sports officially became Nike, named after the Greek goddess of victory.


From there, Nike embarked on a remarkable journey of growth and innovation. The iconic Nike "swoosh" logo was introduced, and the brand quickly gained recognition for its dedication to performance and pushing the limits of athletic footwear. Nike's innovative products, such as the Air Max cushioning system and Flyknit technology, revolutionized the industry and propelled the company to the forefront of athletic apparel and footwear.
